<p><p>The e-bike drive system market features three primary configurations: front-drive, mid-drive, and rear-drive. Front-drive systems integrate motors in the front wheel, offering stable handling and easier maneuverability but can affect weight distribution. Mid-drive systems place the motor at the bike's center, optimizing balance and efficiency, particularly on steep terrain. Rear-drive systems mount the motor on the rear wheel, providing excellent traction and a natural riding feel, making them popular for various cycling conditions. Each type caters to distinct rider preferences and needs.</p></p>
