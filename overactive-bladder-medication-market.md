<p><p>The Overactive Bladder (OAB) medication market includes several key types: anticholinergics, Mirabegron, and Botox. Anticholinergics work by blocking nerve signals that cause muscle contractions in the bladder, effectively reducing urgency and frequency of urination. Mirabegron is a beta-3 adrenergic agonist that relaxes the bladder muscle, enhancing storage capacity. Botox injections temporarily paralyze bladder muscles, providing relief from overactivity. Each type offers unique mechanisms and benefits, catering to various patient needs and treatment preferences in managing OAB symptoms.</p></p>
