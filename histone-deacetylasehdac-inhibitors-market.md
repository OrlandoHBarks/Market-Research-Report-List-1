<p><p>Histone Deacetylase (HDAC) inhibitors are a class of compounds that interfere with the function of histone deacetylases, enzymes involved in modifying histones, which play a crucial role in regulating gene expression. By inhibiting these enzymes, HDAC inhibitors can promote the acetylation of histones, leading to a more relaxed chromatin structure and increased gene expression, particularly in cancer cells. This mechanism has made HDAC inhibitors a focus in cancer therapy and other diseases such as neurodegenerative disorders and inflammatory conditions.</p><p>The HDAC inhibitors market is witnessing significant growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of cancer and the expansion of research into their therapeutic potential in various diseases.
